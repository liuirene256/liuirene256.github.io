Magnetar Capital

Software Engineering Intern, 2024

Magnetar is a leading alternative asset management hedge fund. At this internship, I build an automated monitoring system to verify the delivery of outsourced holdings and market data files. The program was first deployed on Docker and then run with a task scheduler to regularly check that files were delivered at the expected time and if missing files were delivered later on. I equipped the program with Simple Mail Transfer Protocol (SMTP) to alert deliverers of missing files to cover all needs of the firm. The program decreased latency by reducing delivery delays and back-office overtime toil and gave me exposure to C#, Docker, Kubernetes, YAML, and SMTP. Additionally, I had the opportunity to build a professional network with leaders of the firm and attended industry workshops and webinars.



Panduit

Firmware Engineering Intern, 2024

Panduit is a provider of networking and electrical solutions. Here, I developed the PDUs' communication protocol (SNMP) to accommodate multiple administrative users. Doing so involved coming up with clever implementations that support parallel database retrievals and modifications to and from the management information base (MIB). By leveraging C's unique memory management, I was able to incorporate this new feature, making the PDUs more desirable and applicable for larger tech companies. Working at Panduit gave me extensive exposure with Linux, Git & Bitbucket, and C programming as well as experience working on large codebases with other programmers.



Feinberg School of Medicine

Researcher, 2021-Present

I led 2 major research projects as first author with Dr. Claus-Peter Richter. For the first, we aimed to reduce diagnosis cost and increase diagnosis timeliness and accessability in primary care. To do so, I wrote signal processing and machine learning algorithms using MATLAB and Python to detect a nasal disease called Chronic Rhinosinusitis based on speech with >73% accuracy. For the second project, I wrote code to automatically parse and analyze 1000+ X-ray fluorescence microscopy scans. Then, collaborating with the lab, we ran statistical analysis to detect the effectiveness of biphosphonate treatment for Osteogenesis Imperfecta. Both projects were presented at prestigious conferences (ARO and ABRCMS) and are submitted for peer-review.



University of Chicago

Summer Intern, 2022

As a summer intern in UChicago's Security, Algorithms, Networks and Data Lab, I worked on the PimEyes project under Prof. Ben Zhao and with Emily Wenger. I designed a deep learning face generation program to create 20+ deepfakes to measure the scope of PimEyes' image scraping capabilities. For measuring purposes, I built honeypot website and social media accounts to be scraped. Additionally, I presented findings with the lab and assisted other adhoc lab research.
