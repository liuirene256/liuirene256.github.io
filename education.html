Spring 2024 (Dean's List)

  • 15-251: Great Ideas in Theoretical Computer Science ðŸ©·

    • Difficulty: 4/5

    Overall: A lot of this class is just letting your brain marinate on the content you've injested, but it's very rewarding and you can definitely feel your brain grow as the class progresses. Content was super interesting and dealt with many abstract topics. This was definitely the hardest class of my freshman year but the TAs and professors are super accommodating, helpful, and intelligent.

    Exams: Very difficult for me. Some questions would have multiple versions of different difficulties and point values; I would suggest thinking through the lower point value ones first and sketch a solution before doing the full point questions unless you're very confident in your answer.

    Homeworks: Difficult but fun. You write up the solutions in a timed, proctored environment called a writing session, but the questions are posted ahead of time so really you have a lot of time to think. You will surely become very close to the group you choose, and other people in the class, as you guys struggle together.

  • 21-270: Introduction to Mathematical Finance ðŸŒŸ

    • Difficulty: 3/5

    Overall: I loved the professor's (Dr. David Handron) teaching. Definitely needed a lot of help from the TAs to get used to the content and understand the purpose behind the strategies we learned.

    Exams: Ran out of time on all the midterms and the final. There's multiple choice, but those were the hardest questions.

    Homework: Alright and completely reasonable. Some of the very difficult problems were graded for completion after the fact.

    Overall: Needed to take this class to declare AI major. Many people dropped. Workload and difficulty were both reasonable.

Fall 2023 (Dean's List)

  • 15-151: Mathematical Foundations of Computer Science ðŸ©·

    • Difficulty: 1/5

    I LOVED THIS CLASS. WHOLEHEARTEDLY. Professor (John Mackey) is genuinely the best lecturer and an incredibly interesting person. The content (discrete math at other universities plus some extra fun stuff) was also transformative and formalized everything I knew about CS, problem solving, and technical communication. Also served as an introduction to LaTeX which I love almost as much as I loved this class. Met some of my favorite people in this class, and also bonded with others later on over our love for 15-151. Ended up TAing the semester after.

    Midterms are curved and were very fun for me. If you understand the content, they're light, but if not, they serve as a good reminder to spend more time with the material.

    Homeworks were so fun. I loved LaTeXing it all up and creating beauty from mathematics. There were also optional bonus problems that had some interesting tricks to them.

  • 15-122: Principles of Imperative Computation ðŸŒŸ

    • Difficulty: 2/5

    Basically your average data structures and algorithms class. The content carried me through many OAs and technical interviews (who needs leetcode when you have 122)!

    Ran out of time, especially on the first midterm. But they are curved in the sense that questions many people get wrong are weighted less, so in the end my grades were a lot higher than I expected. Questions were pretty reasonable and reflective of past homework assignments.

    Homeworks were pretty straightforward and in C. They do take some time / are quite long but are doable and reflective of the programming we do in labs. I spent more time helping non-CS friends with assignments than I spent doing them myself.

  • 21-242: Matrix Theory

    • Difficulty: 4/5

    Overall: Took a placement exam in the beginning of the year and qualified for honors linear algebra (matrix theory). Class was small so I was able to get a lot of one-on-one help from the professor and the TAs. This was the first class I really struggled in so I didn't have the best experience, but it prepared me very well for future classes in that I learned how to ask for help and prepare for difficult exams.

    Exams: Each test had one proof problem that I could never fully get. The computational problems were alright though and carried my grade. Was this professors first time teaching, though, so this might change.

    Homeworks: All I can say is that I did 90% of the homework in OH. Questions were difficult, the proofs for them even harder. The grading is lenient though.

  • 21-295: Putnam Seminar

    • Highly recommend! Light workload and Poh is a great professor.

  • 33-104: Experimental Physics

    • Difficulty: 2/5

    Overall: Was a night class which is definitely not for me, especially in winter. It felt like this class made me miss out on a lot of fun get togethers with friends and the labs were questionable. Professor was extraordinarily kind.

    Lab Notebooks: These took way too much time and was all busy work. However, I think all the lab requirements are like this. They graded really leniently except for the second notebook which was ultimately curved but made me question everything.
